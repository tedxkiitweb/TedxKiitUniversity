Dr. Achyuta Samanta & KISS

Achyuta Samanta A resilient personality who has embarked on the journey of overcoming obstacles, the life narrative of Prof. Achyuta Samanta reads like a stirring tale of tenacity, tenacity, and social responsibility. He was born and raised in abject poverty in a rural hamlet in Odisha. At the young age of four, he suffered the painful loss of his father, which set off a 15-year quest for survival and education. Nevertheless, he persisted, and at 22, he began teaching. At age 25, he set out on a quest that would forever alter his own life and the lives of countless others. He founded the Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT) and the Kalinga Institute of Social Sciences (KISS) in two rented homes with just Rs. 5000 in his pocket.

Establishing the two institutes wasn’t just a mission but the manifestation of a dream with a purpose. These institutions now serve as rays of hope, offering top-notch education and adorned with every award and repute imaginable nationally and internationally. Prof. Samanta has made enormous contributions to art, culture, sculpture, literature, film, media, spirituality, rural development, education, and the advancement of tribal people. His humanitarian work for society is very remarkable. His impact on sports has been nothing short of extraordinary. By fostering 12 Olympians, building a sizable sports infrastructure, and promoting athletes through his organisations, he has helped to promote physical health, national cohesion, and social integration. His charitable work has influenced innumerable lives. He is a bachelor who exemplifies humility and compassion, despite all his accomplishments, awards, and access to luxuries. He also leads a modest life. With multiple honorary doctorate degrees and innumerable accolades to his name, he is a paragon of how life can blossom if an individual so chooses to nurture the qualities of determination and perseverance combined with hard work. His unwavering dedication to advancing society, in both his roles as a legislator and a social activist, is an inspiration to one and all.

Kalinga Institute of Social Sciences – KISS The Kalinga Institute of Social Sciences (KISS) is a residential school in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, providing free education, lodging and nourishment, healthcare, and vocational, athletic, and artistic training to indigenous children from 62 different tribal groups. A manifestation of the Founder, Prof. (Dr.) Achyuta Samanta’s philanthropic vision, KISS boasts of 30,000 students in KISS, Bhubaneswar, over 15,000 successfully established alumni, and 15000 students in 10 other satellite centres of KISS. Starting as a small initiative with only 125 students in the fiscal year 1992-1993, KISS has always upheld its objective of providing a decent life full of possibilities and opportunities for underprivileged children with immense potential.

Since its inception 28 years ago, KISS has initiated a revolution among the tribal population of Odisha and, by extension, India by eradicating poverty through education and learning. Bestowed with the honour of being the first exclusive tribal Deemed to be University in the world, as accorded by the Ministry of Human Resources Development, Government of India, KISS stands for Gender Equity and Equality, Vocational Empowerment, and Women Empowerment, KISS enjoys Special Consultative Status since 2015, thanks to UN-ECOSOC. Based on the 3E formula: ‘Enable, Educate, and Empower’, KISS has resiliently and silently intervened to stop dropouts, child marriage, gender-based harassment, left-wing extremism, proselytising, illiteracy, and superstition on a massive scale through education and empowerment.

                        

Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology

(Deemed to be University)

Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT), one of India’s prestigious universities, is renowned for its research and teaching quality. In a rented home with 12 students and 2 teachers, it was founded as an industrial training institute in Bhubaneswar in 1992–1993. Prof. Achyuta Samanta envisioned a school that prioritises academic quality and provides innumerable opportunities for students to obtain in-depth information in an era of unpopular education and cautious acceptance of globalisation.


Respected everywhere for the calibre of its academic programmes, research opportunities, and involvement in the local community and its reputation as a school of compassion and humanitarianism, KIIT Deemed to be University has established itself as one of the Leading Education Centers in India thanks to the commitment, tenacity, and resilience of our Founder and the contribution of the hard-working team and well-wishers.


No other educational establishment in India has expanded in scope and scale as much in just 25 years as KIIT has, therefore it has become a paragon of growth. Incredibly, it has undergone a trip from soil to silver. The Limca Book of Records proudly lists KIIT as the first Institute to earn the distinction of achieving University status in less than 6 years. According to the Ministry of Education’s ARIAA, 2022, it is ranked first among all self-financing universities in the nation.

With a varied student body from 65 different countries and throughout India, KIIT offers more than 115 academic programmes through its 23 schools, and more than 195 foreign organisations engage with it daily. KIIT, in virtually every area of interest, has a long history of hosting countless international and national seminars, conferences, workshops, and even significant sporting events.

                        

TED

TED which stands for Technology, Entertainment, and Design, is a media organisation which hosts inspirational and empowering talks emphasising the slogan of “ideas worth spreading”. Richard Saul Wurman came up with the idea of a TED conference in February 1984 as a one-time event. Six years later, it was reinvented as a four-day conference in Monterey, California. By 2014, the TED conferences began a run in Vancouver, Canada, reflecting its growing international appeal.

TED’s early emphasis was on technology and design, consistent with its Silicon Valley origins, but it has since broadened its repertoire to include talks on many scientific, cultural, and academic topics. It is owned by Chris Anderson, a British American businessman, through the Sapling Foundation.


Over time, the organisation has successfully morphed itself into an international circle with an all-encompassing audience that welcomes the idea of harbouring change. The annual TED conference intends to acknowledge and propagate the effectiveness of simple ideas. By sharing the journeys of people who have carved their niche in the world, the conference aims at acknowledging the importance of soldiering through the hurdles in one’s path.
